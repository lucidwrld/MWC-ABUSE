Abuse Topics

Physical Abuse

Involves the intentional use of force causing injury or bodily harm. This can include hitting, slapping, punching, or any form of physical harm.

Emotional Abuse

Inflicts emotional trauma through manipulation, humiliation, intimidation, or constant criticism. It undermines an individual's self-worth and mental well-being.

Sexual Abuse

Involves non-consensual sexual activities or coercion, including rape, molestation, or any unwanted sexual advances. This type of abuse violates personal boundaries and can have severe psychological consequences.

Child Abuse

Encompasses physical, emotional, or sexual maltreatment of a child. Child abuse can impede healthy development and leave lasting emotional scars.

Verbal Abuse

Involves the use of words to belittle, degrade, or threaten. Verbal abuse can include yelling, name-calling, and constant criticism, eroding an individual's self-esteem.

Elder Abuse

Involves harming or exploiting an older person, often in a caregiving or familial relationship. Elder abuse can manifest as physical, emotional, financial, or neglectful mistreatment.

Stalking

Persistent and unwanted attention, harassment, or surveillance directed towards an individual. Stalking can occur in person or through various forms of communication, such as online stalking.

Spiritual Abuse

Manipulation or misuse of spiritual beliefs and practices to control or harm others. This form of abuse can involve coercive religious practices, exploitation of faith, or the imposition of spiritual authority for harmful purposes.
